Who we are

A community that refuses to leave its most vulnerable behind

OBOMOCARE was founded in 2020 by members of the Gusii community who witnessed at close range the compounding hardships facing the most vulnerable households in Kisii and Nyamira Counties. The elderly, the chronically ill, persons living with disabilities, and households headed by orphaned children were living without food, without transport to health facilities, without personal care, and without companionship.

The name comes from the Gusii word obomo — nurturing warmth, the sustaining care a parent gives a child. That word is both our name and our mission: dignified, practical, integrated support as a matter of right, not charity.

For five years we have operated entirely on community-generated resources — personal contributions, supermarket donations, church networks, and Harambee fundraising. We have served over two thousand families. Now we are ready to scale.

Our work

Four pillars. One integrated model.

We don't choose between food or healthcare or dignity. The needs don't arrive separately — and neither do our interventions.

Food support

A fortnightly OBOMOCARE Food Basket delivered to enrolled households, plus meal coordination for elderly persons who cannot cook independently. Sourced through supermarket, business, and church partnerships.

Transport facilitation

Formal partnerships with boda boda associations ensure enrolled beneficiaries reach health facilities, attend clinic appointments, and maintain medication regimens — removing the cost and physical barrier of rural transport.

Personal care and dignity

Trained volunteer caregivers provide bathing, dressing, grooming, wound care, and hygiene support at the household level. Dignity is not a luxury. It is a right. Every visit is documented to catch deterioration early.

Companionship and inclusion

Structured social engagement — conversation, games, storytelling, accompaniment to community events — combats isolation that accelerates cognitive decline and withdrawal. We have seen it restore people's will to live fully.

The context

Why the Gusii Region, and why now

Kisii and Nyamira Counties are home to nearly 1.9 million people. Food poverty rates exceed the national average. Healthcare access barriers are acute. And the people most at risk fall through every gap.

Food poverty

39.7% food poverty in Kisii County and 35.9% in Nyamira — both significantly above Kenya's national rate. Chronic malnutrition weakens immunity and worsens disease.

Healthcare access barriers

A boda boda trip to a clinic represents a significant share of daily household income. Missed appointments lead to treatment failure. Kenya records 35,000+ infant deaths annually — largely in rural communities.

The caregiving gap

As working-age adults migrate to cities, elderly parents are left without daily care. Without personal support, hygiene-related conditions develop, dignity erodes, and isolation deepens.

Social isolation

Many individuals OBOMOCARE serves have no regular visitor. This isolation accelerates cognitive decline, deepens depression, and reduces engagement with health and community systems.

Structural absence

Kenya's formal health system operates at facility level. Social protection programmes don't reach the majority of households OBOMOCARE serves. Community Health Promoters lack logistical and material support.

Compounding cycles

Food insecurity weakens immunity → illness increases → healthcare is inaccessible → treatment fails → caregiving burden grows → isolation deepens. Each problem amplifies the others. Our four pillars interrupt all four simultaneously.

Volunteer caregiver corps

Forty trained caregivers. One community.

Our volunteer caregivers are the heart of OBOMOCARE. Drawn from the communities they serve, trained to a professional standard, and supported to stay.

The programme will train and deploy 40 community volunteers drawn from the target sub-counties. Training is delivered in partnership with a qualified health institution and covers:

  • Personal care techniques — bathing, dressing, grooming, wound care
  • Safeguarding and dignity principles
  • Basic health literacy and referral documentation
  • First Aid and CPR
  • Waste management
  • Basic mental health support
  • Documentation and communication skills

A career pathway, not just a volunteer role

Volunteers receive a monthly support stipend, transport reimbursements, peer support groups, and formal recognition. Skills gained through the programme qualify volunteers for progression into community health assistant roles within the county government system.

Governance and accountability

Structured from the start

OBOMOCARE is governed by a constitution adopted at its inaugural General Assembly. Authority is distributed across six independent organs with clear mandates and checks.

General Assembly

Supreme decision-making organ. All registered members in good standing. Adopts constitution, elects trustees, approves annual accounts.

Supreme organ

Board of Trustees

Five to nine members elected for 3-year terms. Responsible for strategic direction, financial oversight, and ensuring legal compliance.

Meets quarterly

Executive Committee

Nine elected officers including Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, Programmes Officer, Volunteer Coordinator, and Community Liaison Officer.

Meets monthly

Programmes Committee

Oversight and strategic guidance on all four programme pillars. Submits quarterly programme reports to the Board of Trustees.

Oversight body

Finance and Audit Committee

Reviews all financial statements before Board presentation. Annual external audit by independent qualified auditor. Full financial transparency to all donors.

Annual audit

Disciplinary Committee

Independent body handling all disciplinary matters. No Executive Committee member may sit on it. Operates free from interference by any other organ.

Independent
